## Compliance guardrails inferred from product material

Source checked locally from the product insert:

- official support language includes skin hydration, glossy coat, general comfort, and antioxidant support
- usage direction says spray over food or directly in the mouth
- product insert says `1 spray per 5 lb / 2.27 kg of body weight daily`
- the insert also carries the U.S. disclaimer that the product is not intended to diagnose, treat, cure, or prevent disease

Keep future copy inside these lines:

- use `supports`, `helps maintain`, `promotes`, `helps keep`
- avoid cure, relief, treatment, anti-itch, anti-inflammatory, allergy, infection, dermatitis, or medical before/after claims
- keep U.S. market framing if these assets stay in the same series

## Design 4 direction options

Option A - `Daily routine simplicity` (recommended)

- Why this fits: the current campaign has benefits, ingredient education, and playful/social angles, but it is missing the easiest conversion message: simple daily use.
- Best use: middle-of-funnel content that makes the product feel easy, low-friction, and compatible with normal pet routines.

Option B - `Seasonal dry skin support`

- Why it fits: it is commercially strong and easy to understand.
- Tradeoff: it gets close to symptom language and needs more careful compliance handling.

Option C - `Owner lifestyle / premium pet care`

- Why it fits: it matches the polished visual style already used in `Design 5`.
- Tradeoff: it is weaker educationally and overlaps more with the playful assets already done.

Recommendation: proceed with Option A.
